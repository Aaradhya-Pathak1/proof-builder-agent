Activity going in:
I designed an analog IC project in Cadence Virtuoso on the gpdk180 (180nm) PDK solo, taking a MOSFET from device characterization through amplifier and filter design. I first characterized an NMOS by sweeping Id vs Vgs to extract the threshold voltage (~0.5V) and the channel-length-modulation parameter lambda, comparing a 180nm device (~0.21) against a 540nm one (~0.055). I then designed a resistive-load common-source amplifier to a hard spec (180uW, Class A, Vov 0.2V), working the bias math and iteratively sweeping transistor width until Vout hit 1V (W = 4.9u, L = 400n), and confirmed the Q-point with a load-line plot. I extracted the small-signal gain three ways and reconciled them: by hand (~5.95), from the VTC slope (-5.59, converging to -5.945 at a 1mV step), and from an AC transient measurement. I drove a 300mV input to force clipping, ran a PSS spectral analysis over 10 harmonics, and plotted THD vs Vin. I then built four more single-stage topologies (diode-load, triode-load, current-source-load, active-load, plus source degeneration), deriving and simulating each gain expression, and finished with RC low-pass, high-pass, and band-pass filters, using AC analysis to verify the -3dB cutoff at 159Hz and the -20dB/decade single-pole roll-off.
